# Portfolio Project Brief

## Premium Personal Brand Website for Jomel Noriega

This document defines the vision, strategy, and design direction for transforming the existing portfolio into a premium personal brand website for Jomel Noriega.

---

## 1. Brand Identity

### Name
Jomel Noriega

### Role
GoHighLevel CRM & Automation Specialist

### Core Message
I help service-based businesses automate lead follow-up, appointment booking, customer communication, and CRM workflows using GoHighLevel.

### Audience
- Business owners
- Marketing agencies
- Recruiters
- Potential employers
- Upwork and Fiverr clients

---

## 2. Core Goal

The website should immediately communicate:

- Trust
- Professionalism
- Technical ability
- Attention to detail

When someone lands on the site, they should think:

- This person is professional.
- This person knows GoHighLevel.
- I’d trust this person with my CRM.
- I want to work with Jomel.

---

## 3. Design Direction

The visual direction should feel premium, modern, and intentional.

### Inspiration
Comparable in feel to:

- Linear
- Vercel
- Stripe
- Raycast
- Notion
- Apple

### Desired Style
- Minimal
- Premium
- Modern
- Clean
- Technical
- Trustworthy
- Professional

### Avoid
- Generic agency templates
- Stock illustrations
- Loud gradients
- Excessive glow effects
- Overly colorful sections
- Clutter

Everything should feel deliberate and polished.

---

## 4. UX and Layout Goals

The site should feel like a refined digital presence, not a generic portfolio.

### Priorities
- Improve visual hierarchy
- Refine section spacing
- Improve alignment and rhythm
- Strengthen typography
- Improve CTA placement
- Create a smoother overall flow

### Experience Goal
Each section should naturally lead into the next, guiding visitors from introduction to trust to action.

---

## 5. Visual System

The design system should feel consistent and elevated throughout.

### Use Consistent Elements
- Typography
- Colors
- Spacing
- Border radius
- Shadows
- Icons
- Buttons
- Cards

### Visual Refinements
- Increase whitespace throughout the site
- Use subtle shadows on cards
- Use modern rounded corners
- Make buttons feel premium
- Use tasteful gradients only where appropriate

---

## 6. Motion and Interaction

Subtle motion should reinforce the premium feel without becoming distracting.

### Suggested Motion
- Fade-ins
- Slide-ins
- Hover animations
- Card interactions
- Button transitions

### Principles
- Smooth
- Premium
- Minimal
- Never excessive

---

## 7. Responsiveness

The site must feel polished across all device sizes.

### Target Screen Sizes
- Desktop
- Tablet
- Mobile

### Focus Areas
- Spacing adjustments
- Typography scaling
- Navigation usability
- CTA accessibility

---

## 8. Copywriting Direction

The messaging should be client-focused and outcome-driven.

### Writing Style
Clear, direct, and professional.

### Replace Feature-Based Language
Instead of saying:
- “I build workflows”

Prefer messaging like:
- “Automate your business so you can focus on growth.”

### CTA Recommendations
- View Case Study
- Let’s Build Your CRM
- Book a Discovery Call

### Messaging Goal
The copy should focus on business outcomes, efficiency, trust, and growth.

---

## 9. Personal Brand Positioning

This website should represent Jomel as a personal brand, not as an agency.

### Brand Focus
- Jomel Noriega
- GoHighLevel CRM & Automation Specialist

### Important Constraint
Do not refer to LiftFlow.

The brand should feel personal, credible, and direct.

---

## 10. Case Study Strategy

The Dental Clinic project should be the hero case study.

### Case Study Presentation
Present it professionally and clearly.

### Include
- Problem
- Solution
- Workflow
- Technologies
- Results
- Screenshots
- Automation flow

### Purpose
Recruiters and clients should immediately understand the depth of the work and the business impact.

---

## 11. Trust and Credibility

The website should increase confidence by showcasing expertise and process.

### Highlight
- Certifications
- Portfolio projects
- Technical skills
- Customer service background
- Process
- Testing methodology

### Important Message
Every workflow should be tested thoroughly before deployment.

This reinforces reliability and professionalism.

---

## 12. Performance and Accessibility

The final website should remain lightweight and accessible.

### Goals
- Keep the site fast
- Avoid unnecessary libraries
- Maintain strong accessibility standards
- Preserve a polished experience across devices

---

## 13. Final Objective

The completed website should feel refined enough to impress:

- Senior recruiters
- Business owners
- Agencies
- Potential clients

It should present Jomel as a serious, capable, and trustworthy specialist in GoHighLevel automation.

---

## 14. Implementation Direction

The existing project should be improved rather than rebuilt from scratch.

### Approach
- Preserve what already works
- Refine the current structure and content
- Improve layout, typography, spacing, and motion
- Strengthen the personal brand presentation
- Make the website feel premium and conversion-oriented

---

## 15. Success Criteria

The project is successful when the site feels:

- Premium
- Professional
- Clear
- Confident
- Trustworthy
- Conversion-focused

And it clearly positions Jomel as the right person to handle CRM and automation work.
